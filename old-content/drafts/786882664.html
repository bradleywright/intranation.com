The quest for epic burgers

==============================
Experiments in burger making
==============================

Inspired by a recent discussion with `a friend `_, and a lazy summer afternoon in London, I decided (much to `my fianceé’s `_ disgust) to cook several kinds of burgers in the American style.

Taking my inspiration from `a Hamburger Today `_, I set out to create three distinct burger styles:

* A `smash burger `_, which is a super-thin fast food-alike burger;
* A `slider `_, in the style of famous American burger chain `White Castle >`_; and
* A standard, slightly thicker burger in the pub style, but with the addition of `Salt and Fat’s divot `_ to see if I could stop it falling apart.

All the burgers were made using just fresh mince from my local butcher and some salt.

The Smash
=========

The Smash is a currently popular style due to its likeness to standard fast food burgers, but it’s typically made with better ingredients. To follow along, try this `full slider recipe `_.

Ultimately, as I was making testing burgers, I used a cast iron skillet (like `this one from Amazon `_), and heated it with a teaspoon of oil unti the oil was smoking. Then:

* Roll mince into a golf-ball sized ball, and give it a very slight pat to firm it up;
* Season one side with salt;
* Place the seasoned side down in the skillet, and season the upper side;
* Let it cook for a minute before flipping; and then
* Squash down with your spatchula on the browned circle now present.»»»»
